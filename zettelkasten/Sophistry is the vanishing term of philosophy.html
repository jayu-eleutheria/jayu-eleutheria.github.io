Sophistry is the vanishing term of philosophy

ID: 3
Creation: 2022-12-08
Last modification: 2023-05-19

Tags: #proposition, #reconstruction of psychoanalysis

Barbara Cassin affirms in Jacques le sophiste that the lógos-phármakon posits a difficulty for lógos-being harmony conceptions, as it unveils a certain power of influence language has which does not depend on (and is often incompatible with) truth.

One could say sophistry is a valid occurrence of a vanishing term inside a human field; in this case, philosophy, if Barbara Cassin's conception is correct. It also seems to be one of the possible entryways for an anti-philosophy, which Barbara Cassin's usage of Jacques Lacan suggests.
