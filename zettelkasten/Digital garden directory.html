A collection of sites similar to this Zettelkasten which I like.

  • Anagora: A collaborative digital garden. Differently from a regular wiki, in which users edit a single article, Anagora users collaborate with their own individual, independent notes—called "subnodes" in Anagora. Subnodes with the same title are aggregated (instead of combined) inside a node—a fancy tagging system, basically. Wikilinks are directed to nodes instead of subnodes.
  • Agency Made Me Do It: A really interesting personal wiki circling around the subject of agency. Its content is mostly philosophical or philosophy-adjacent.
  • Jon Sterling's Forest: A great example of how to organize STEM knowledge (in this case, mathematical research) less vertically.
  • ThoughtStorms: An old semi-personal wiki which has composted itself after a while and nowadays lives a second life. Eclectic, but has a good amount of interesting notes on politics.
  • Neil's Digital Garden: One of Anagora's users. Eclectic, but has a lot of interesting content about the politics of tech.
  • The Garden of Maggie Appleton: A well-known digital gardener talking about digital gardening itself and adjacent topics. Slow, but high-quality.
